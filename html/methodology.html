RANKING METHODOLOGY

In furtherance of her quest for increased citizens participation in governance in a manner that promotes transparency and accountability in public finance management, the Public and Private Development Centre (PPDC) has expanded the scope of her anti-corruption program beyond the Federal Government by working closely with relevant stakeholders at the sub-national level to commence the “Open State Government Ranking” (OSGR).

The OSGR will be informed and guided by the level of State Governments’ commitment towards the promotion of transparency and accountability whilst increasing citizens participation in governance by making public information available through publication on its website. The criteria(s) for the ranking are listed below:

  1. States that have signed up to the Open Government Partnership (OGP)
  2. Availability of State Budget on the State’s official website ( specifically looking at the last 3 years i.e 2016, 2017 and 2018 Appropriation Act)
  3. Availability of State Contractual Information on the States website

RANKING METHODOLOGY (CRITERIA)

  • Signing up to the Open Government Partnership (OGP) - The Open Government Partnership is a multilateral initiative that aims to secure concrete commitments from national and subnational governments to promote open government, empower citizens, fight corruption, and harness new technologies to strengthen governance. At the 2016 anti corruption summit held in London, President Muhammadu Buhari reaffirmed his administration's commitment in the fight against corruption by signing up to OGP at the national level. Such commitment is equally desirable at the subnational level and States are encouraged to key into the program. Accordingly, a State will score sixty (60) points upon signing to the program.
    Signed Up to the OGP = 20 Points
    Not Signed to the OGP = 0 Points
  • Availability of State Budget on the website (specifically looking at the last 3 years i.e 2016, 2017 and 2018 Appropriation Act): A state with her budget online would be assessed on a score of 20 points. Note that the ranking process would accord more preference and points to states who have uploaded at least the preceding 3 years budget online.
    State with Budget Information on her Website = 20 Points
    No Available Budget Information = 0 Points
  • Availability of State Contractual Information on the States website - A state with this information on her website will score 20 points as well. Contractual information under this criteria is defined as the contractual details of each project carried out by the state each year and the information shall include the location of the project, contract amount and the project contractor. A state with only pictures of projects online will not score any point. It is equally important to note that number of years of the contractual information a state have on its website will rank it above a state with lesser number of years.
    Available Contractual Information = 20 Points
    No available Contractual Information = 0 Points

In conclusion, states will be assessed and ranked according to their performance evidencing their commitment towards a transparent and accountable system of government .

The Open State Government Ranking is an initiative of the Public and Private Development Centre with support from AccessNG
